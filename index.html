My Journey in Operations and Supply Chain Management

Hello! I’m Yadnyesh L. Khapekar, currently pursuing an MBA in Operations and Supply Chain Management at MIT-WPU. I started this program in August 2024 with the goal of building expertise in a field that plays a vital role in every business’s success. I’m excited to share my journey with you through this blog, where I’ll explore what I’m learning, industry trends, and my insights into the evolving world of supply chain management.

  • Why I Chose Operations and Supply Chain Management Every product we buy, every service we use, has a complex journey behind it, involving planning, procurement, production, and logistics. This seamless integration of processes fascinates me, and it’s a big reason why I chose this field. Operations and supply chain management isn’t just about moving goods; it’s about driving efficiency, improving service quality, and responding to the fast-evolving demands of today’s global markets.
  • My Experience So Far Since beginning my MBA, I’ve had numerous opportunities to grow, both academically and personally. Early on, I was welcomed into the Operations and Supply Chain Management forum after winning an Ice Breaking session—an experience that motivated me to dive deeper into my studies. I’ve also had a memorable interaction with my lecturer, Shreya Shah, who makes learning both engaging and practical through interactive activities and real-world examples.
  • What’s Next Looking ahead, I’m eager to apply what I’m learning in a professional setting, where I can contribute to creating efficient, sustainable, and resilient supply chains. I believe that a strong supply chain is a crucial foundation for any business, and I’m excited to be part of a field that directly impacts customer satisfaction, cost management, and business continuity.

My Experience with the Operations Forum at MIT-WPU

Hello, and welcome to my portfolio! I’m Yadnyesh L. Khapekar, a current MBA student specializing in Operations and Supply Chain Management at MIT-WPU. One of the highlights of my journey so far has been my involvement with the Operations Forum, a dedicated platform for students like me who are passionate about the field of operations. In this blog, I’ll share how the forum has enriched my academic experience, contributed to my growth, and shaped my career aspirations.

  • What is the Operations Forum? The Operations Forum at MIT-WPU is a collaborative space where students come together to discuss, explore, and gain practical insights into operations and supply chain management. Through workshops, guest lectures, case study discussions, and interactive activities, the forum bridges the gap between academic learning and real-world application. It’s a place where we can delve into the complexities of the field, discuss industry trends, and learn from experts.
  • Learning Beyond the Classroom The Operations Forum provides a platform for experiential learning beyond traditional lectures and assignments. Through case studies and simulations, I’ve had the chance to apply theoretical concepts to practical scenarios. For instance, recent discussions on topics like lean manufacturing and supply chain resilience helped me understand the real-world challenges businesses face in maintaining efficiency and agility..
  • Looking Ahead As I continue my journey with the Operations Forum, I’m excited to dive deeper into advanced topics, expand my knowledge of emerging technologies, and further develop my leadership skills. I’m grateful for the opportunity to learn in such an inspiring environment, and I’m looking forward to sharing more experiences, projects, and insights on this blog as I progress.
